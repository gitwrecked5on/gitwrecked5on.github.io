Hi!

 I'm AV, welcome to my Chaos Garden. I've spent my career building processes and systems from the ground up across ecommerce, fintech, and social impact - almost exclusively in chaotic 0-to-1 environments. My work has usually been to inhabit the intersection of technology, business, and finance, figure out what needs to exist and then keep tinkering till existence is achieved. Constantly building, breaking, learning; Occasionally in that order.

Chaotic and unstructured thinking feel more natural to me because I truly believe thinking isn't a linear process. Thinking, much like any other process, will never beat entropy. Rather than brute-forcing order on chaos, I am trying to now.. cultivate it.

>*Chaos gardening is a popular, low-maintenance trend involving scattering mixed seeds randomly -often from old, partial packets - to grow a wild, unexpected garden. This “survival of the fittest” method saves money and effort, [creating a vibrant, naturally chaotic look](https://cdn.mos.cms.futurecdn.net/HDsFWr4NbVqGPBrsBXNRym-1024-80.jpg.webp). It encourages utilizing small spaces or containers for a surprise harvest of flowers and vegetables.

I chose this name to document "how my brain does braining" because it closely relates to the intriguing  concept of [Digital Gardens](https://jzhao.xyz/posts/networked-thought), something I came across when learning how to self-host a website, and ties in deeply to the way I process information.

What is a digital garden?

> *A digital garden is not a file cabinet, nor is it fully an index. A digital garden is less so a well-kempt plot for farming and more a mess of entangled growth. It is a network of interconnected ideas and thoughts, clustered by how they are associated with each other.*

My ~~Digital~~  Chaos Garden is a hub of half-finished thoughts, stories around things I've built,  and learnings from things I've broken. Expect ~~raandomly scribbled notes~~ curated posts on products and systems, fintech, local-first tech, data pipelines, and just general musings. 

These are a few excerpts from a larger brain dump that I pretend to organize frequently and succeed at occasionally.
