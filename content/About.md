#skeleton


I call this a Chaos Garden.

It’s not carefully planned. Things get added as they come—notes from past work, fragments of what I’m thinking about now, ideas that aren’t fully formed yet. Some connect, some don’t. Over time, a shape starts to appear, but it isn’t forced.

The name comes from a kind of garden where nothing is arranged too strictly. Seeds are scattered and left to grow on their own terms. Some take hold, some fade out, and what survives isn’t always what you’d expect. I’m not into botany, but I like the idea of letting things find their place instead of deciding it upfront.
